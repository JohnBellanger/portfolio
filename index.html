Facebook Ads Campaign Performance

View Dashboard View Presentation SQL Code

1. Context and Objective of the Stakeholder

The objective of this mission, at the request of my stakeholder, was to understand why increasing advertising spending on Facebook Ads was not translating into equivalent growth in the company's overall revenue. The stakeholder noted that profitability appeared to be at risk, particularly in certain quarters.

The hypothesis raised was that traffic from Facebook Ads was of low quality, leading to overestimation of conversions by the platform itself, a low conversion rate on the site, and a low value per customer.

2. The Problem

The analysis confirmed the stakeholder's concerns: an overall low ROAS (Return On Ad Spend) for Facebook traffic, and a conversion rate for this same channel that is the lowest of all acquisition channels. A particularly negative ROAS was observed in December, signaling a major underperformance.

ROAS vs CPA Trajectory

The analysis confirmed the stakeholder's concerns: an overall low ROAS for Facebook traffic, and a conversion rate for this same channel that is the lowest of all acquisition channels. A particularly negative ROAS was observed in December, signaling a major underperformance.

3. The Method of Analysis

I adopted a rigorous methodology in several phases to respond to this problem:

  • Data cleaning: I duplicated the raw tables from Facebook Ads and Google Analytics 4 to work on copies on BigQuery. I then standardized date formats, handled missing values, and converted expense columns to a numeric format. Used LOWER() and TRIM() to ensure naming consistency.
  • Analyse Descriptive: I created a flat table by joining the cleaned data. Explored data to find:
  • Location of the anomaly: Toronto in Canada was particularly problematic in terms of profitability.

    • ROAS by City (Toronto Anomaly)

    I analyzed performance by country and city. I observed that the city of Toronto in Canada was particularly problematic in terms of profitability.

  • The month affected: Drastic drop in performance in December, with ROAS turning negative.
  • Campaign affected: "Valentines_Promo" and "Post-Holiday-Sale" were showing very low profitability.

    • Campaign Profitability Comparison (ROAS)

    I reviewed the campaign performance. I noticed that two campaigns, "Valentines_Promo" and "Post-Holiday-Sale," were showing very low profitability.

  • Attribution: Facebook's attribution model overestimated its conversions, attributing 51% more to itself than GA4 did.

4. Diagnostic Analysis

I formulated several hypotheses to understand the cause:

  • Hypothesis 1 (Strategy): Ad strategy not adapted to target audience.
  • Hypothesis 2 (Execution): Incorrect setup/optimization (targeting, high bids).
  • Hypothesis 3 (Customer): Saturated or un-receptive audience in Toronto.
  • Hypothesis 4 (Technical): Pixel/GA4 tracking malfunction.
  • Hypothesis 5 (Content): Creatives/Products not attractive enough.
  • Hypothesis 6 & 7: Competition / Seasonality & Economic factors.

I focused on Hypothesis 1 because the analysis revealed a glaring anomaly.

5. Validation of the problem

Looking at the data for the "Post-Holiday-Sale" campaign, which launched in December 2024, I had an "Aha Moment." The campaign's name clearly indicates that it should have been launched after the holiday season. The data confirmed this error: the campaign was executed at the wrong time, wasting significant budget at a time when the offer was not relevant. This simple observation helped explain the entire underperformance.

Attribution Gap: FB vs GA4 Conversions

I quantified Facebook's contribution to the total acquisition and measured the attribution gap. I found that Facebook's attribution model overestimated its conversions, attributing 51% more to itself than GA4.

6. Recommendations & Quantified Impact

  • Immediate Action: Pause or adjust poorly timed campaigns to avoid budget waste.
  • Optimization: Review the annual marketing calendar with clear launch dates.
  • Preventive Action: Implement a double validation system for campaign launches.

Prediction: Based on the performance of a properly planned winter sales campaign (ROAS 7.19), launching the "Post-Holiday-Sale" at the right time could have generated $3,240 additional income and about 56 conversions for the same budget. This correction would have transformed a loss into a substantial profit.

7. Demonstrated Technical Skills

  • Databases & SQL Logic: Joined Facebook Ads and GA4 to create a flat table, ensuring data standardization.
  • Statistical & Predictive Analysis: Scenario modeling to provide a quantifiable forecast ($3,240 additional revenue).
  • Data visualization: Used Google Sheets to explore anomalies and support conclusions.

2. Data Transformation Methodology

1. Executive Summary

The analysis of Apple's cash position as of September 2025 reveals exceptionally robust financial health and very comfortable short-term liquidity. Key indicators demonstrate a proven ability to cover short-term liabilities.

  • Current Ratio stands at 1.8.
  • Working Capital Requirement (WCR) is negative at -$119,739, acting as a source of financing.
  • Conclusion: Management possesses full strategic flexibility for growth initiatives or deleveraging operations.

2. KPIs and Short-Term Liquidity

Total Net Sales: $1,004,592 | Net Income: $247,948 (Increased from 2024).

Current Ratio: A ratio of 1.8 is considered very healthy. The company can easily cover its entire current liabilities ($165,631M) with its current assets ($281,518M). We observe a positive "scissors" dynamic where assets increase (+3.0%) while liabilities decrease (-6.1%).

Revenue Streams Distribution

iPhone represents the primary revenue driver, contributing 48.5% of Apple's total sales in FY2025.

3. Operating Cycle Analysis

Working Capital Requirement (WCR): A negative WCR (-$119,739) is characteristic of a dominant business model. Apple collects cash from its sales before it must pay its suppliers. The NWC is largely positive and more than covers the WCR, resulting in a structurally positive and growing Net Cash position.

Working Capital Profile

A negative WCR (-$119,739M) acts as a source of cash that funds growth, reflecting a highly efficient operating cycle.

4. Strategic Recommendation

The company's cash position is optimal and under no financial pressure. Decisions can be based on ROI criteria.

  • Option 1: Massive Internal Investment in AI. Apple can afford to invest massively in internal R&D to catch up on its perceived AI deficit.
  • Option 2: Strategic AI Partnership. Partnering (e.g., with Gemini) provides immediate access to capabilities without significant upfront capital.
  • Option 3: Focus on Core Business. Continue investing in product improvement and advertising.

Decision: The choice depends on Apple's risk appetite. This analysis confirms the company has the financial means to pursue any of these initiatives without compromising stability.

View Presentation R Analysis Code

1. Context & Business Problem

How to design a hyper-effective advertising campaign for a back pain solution? Rather than intuition, I wanted to rigorously analyze spontaneous conversations of hundreds of people on public forums (Reddit) to build a surgically precise message.

2. Methodology (R Programming)

Deployed a complete processing pipeline within R:

  • Data Source: Scraped Reddit using Apify.
  • Cleaning: Used tolower(), gsub for punctuation/digits, and removeWords(stopwords) to isolate meaningful terms.
  • Analysis Tool: sentimentr library for sentiment scoring and emotion_by() to detect 8 fundamental emotions.

Detected Emotional Spectrum (Radar)

Sadness and fear are the most frequently expressed emotions by the audience.

3. Analysis & Insights: The "Aha Moment"

The emotional palette is dominated by negativity, fear, and sadness. Key Observation: Back pain is not just a physical issue; it is an emotional suffering that impacts daily life. I tested 3 hypotheses:

  • H1 (Emotional > Physical?): Validated. Pain is linked to sadness and fear. Our ad must speak to these emotions (Empathy).
  • H2 (Attack Competitors?): Invalidated. Sentiment towards medications/doctors is neutral. An aggressive strategy would be ineffective.

    • Competitor Sentiment Distribution

    Neutrality dominates, indicating that an aggressive advertising strategy against competitors would be ill-advised.

  • H3 (Ideal words?): Validated via word cloud. Words like "relief", "walk", "sleep", "finally" are what customers desire.

    • Top Customer Word Associations (Bigram Frequency)

    This word cloud provides the key arguments for our future advertising promise.

4. Action Plan & Predictive Recommendations

1. Immediate Recommendation: Launch an Empathetic Ad Campaign

A T-test predicts this approach will generate a CTR of 2.08% vs 1.22% (+70% increase with 99.7% statistical confidence).

Predicted Ad CTR (T-Test comparison)

The T-test confirms that the empathetic message significantly outperforms the generic approach.

2. Optimization Recommendation: Develop Reassurance Content

Linear Regression predicts content focused on benefits will increase the engagement rate by +2.5 percentage points (p-value: 2.66e-08).

Reassurance Prediction: Number of Articles vs Engagement Increase

Our model predicts a significant increase in engagement thanks to a targeted content strategy.

3. Preventive Recommendation: Build a "Champions" Community

K-means clustering identified high-value segments ($55-$80 average order value, 10-20 purchases/year). Target them for loyalty.

Customer Segmentation (K-Means Clustering)

Clustering reveals a high-value customer segment, a priority target for our loyalty initiatives.

SEO Traffic Drop Diagnosis

View Dashboard View Presentation

1. Background: Why did SEO growth stop?

As an SEO consultant for an artisanal coffee site, I was commissioned to analyze a sudden drop in organic traffic observed from May 2025. The objective: diagnose the root cause and develop an action plan to regain growth momentum.

2. Methodology in Google Sheets

Used Pivot Tables, SUMIFS/AVERAGEIFS, and Linear Regression to quantify the decline and predict impact. The descriptive analysis revealed:

  • Where: Loss concentrated on informational blog posts.

    • Organic Traffic Loss by Page Type

    The loss of traffic is mainly concentrated on the blog posts informational in nature. These are the pages that attracted a large volume of users and are now in difficulty.

  • When: Brutal drop starting May 2025.
  • Who: Performance collapsed ONLY on mobile devices. Desktop remained stable.

Traffic Volume: Mobile vs Desktop Over Time

The drop is clear and brutal. It began in May 2025, breaking with the good momentum of previous months. Our click traffic fell by 341 in April to an average of 28 in May-June.

3. Diagnosing the Cause

Tested hypotheses: internal technical changes, competitor offensives, algorithm updates.

Conclusion (Confirmed): Perfect temporal correlation between the drop and Google's official update on mobile UX (Core Web Vitals). Google penalized our blog pages on mobile for insufficient usability and load times.

4. Quantified Action Plan

Recommendation 1: Stop the Bleed (Immediate Action, 1-2 Weeks)

Fix Core Web Vitals (speed, usability) on the 5 to 10 most affected blog pages immediately. Regression modeling predicts that restoring our initial positions will help us regain over 10,000 monthly clicks.

SEO Position vs Monthly Clicks (Linear Regression Model)

Fix Core Web Vitals (speed, usability) on the 5 to 10 most affected blog pages immediately. Regression modeling predicts that restoring our initial positions will help us regain over 10,000 monthly clicks.

Recommendation 2: Aim for Excellence (Medium Term, 1-2 Months)

Enrich content of strategic pages and improve overall layout. The goal is to increase mobile engagement rate from 45% to 60%, securing an audience of ~30,000 monthly users against future algorithm updates.

Recommendation 3: Quality Assurance (Long Term, Ongoing)

Implement mobile performance monitoring via automated Looker Studio dashboards and QA checklists. This crisis cost approximately 312 clicks per day; this checklist acts as an insurance policy for a future goal of zero traffic loss.

Expatriation Financial Dashboard

View Google Sheets Dashboard

1. Context and Business Problem

As part of a personal project simulating a move to Japan, I set a goal for financial independence. The business problem: build a reliable, automated ETL and BI reporting system capable of consolidating heterogeneous data sources to track my monthly expenses against passive income.

2. Data-Driven Methodology

Utilized Google Sheets as an ETL and BI platform:

  • Data Sources: Monthly manual expense inputs, asset performance tracking sheets.
  • Web Scraping: Used =IMPORTHTML to scrape real-time cost-of-living data for Osaka from Numbeo.com.
  • Consolidation: Used =IMPORTRANGE to centralize data. Solved Master Data Management issues (evolving expense categories) by strictly adding new rows instead of renaming, ensuring historical data integrity.

3. Analysis: Complex Formulas

Created real-time currency conversion formulas pulling data from GOOGLEFINANCE APIs to convert USD revenue into simulated JPY expenses. Engineered a Financial Independence KPI using =SPARKLINE to visually track the percentage of expenses covered by income inside the cells.

4. Dashboard & Results

The master data sheet acts as the single source of truth. With advanced filters, I can explore expenses by category, compare asset performance, and visualize the impact of inflation on my budget over time.
